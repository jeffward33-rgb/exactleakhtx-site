What to Do Right Now If You Have an Active Leak

If water is actively flowing in your home, these steps will minimize damage while you wait for professional help:

1. Shut off your main water supply. In most Houston homes, the main shut-off valve is near the front of the house at ground level, close to the water meter. It's either a round handle you turn clockwise or a lever you turn perpendicular to the pipe. This is the single most important step — it stops the water.

2. Turn off your water heater. With the supply off, your water heater can overheat and get damaged. Switch it off at the breaker (electric) or turn the gas valve to "off" or "pilot" (gas).

3. Move valuables and electronics away from the water. Get important documents, electronics, and irreplaceable items out of the affected area. Every minute counts for water-sensitive belongings.

4. Document everything and call us. Take photos and video of the damage for insurance. Then call (346) 222-3951 — we'll assess the urgency and schedule the fastest possible response.

When You Need Emergency Leak Detection

Not every leak is an emergency — but these situations can't wait.

🌊

Active Water Flooding

Water is actively running, pooling, or flooding an area of your home. Even after shutting off the main, you need the source identified immediately so repairs and mitigation can begin.

💥

Sudden Pipe Burst

A supply line burst — often after a freeze or from corroded pipe failure — can dump hundreds of gallons per hour. Fast detection means fast repair and dramatically less total damage.

🏚️

Ceiling or Wall Collapse Risk

Water-saturated ceiling drywall is heavy and dangerous. If your ceiling is bulging, sagging, or dripping heavily, the situation demands urgent attention before structural failure occurs.

⚡

Water Near Electrical Systems

Water and electricity are a deadly combination. If a leak is near your electrical panel, outlets, or wiring, the safety risk escalates the urgency far beyond property damage concerns.

🏢

Commercial Tenant Emergency

Water damage in occupied commercial or multi-family properties creates habitability and liability issues that can't wait. Tenant safety and spreading damage demand immediate investigation.

🧊

Post-Freeze Pipe Failures

After a Houston freeze, thawing pipes reveal fractures and splits that leak all at once. Post-freeze is one of our busiest emergency periods — call as soon as you spot water.

Water Emergencies in Houston

Houston's climate creates emergency scenarios other cities rarely face. Freeze events — like the ones that crippled the city in recent winters — cause widespread pipe failures across thousands of homes simultaneously. When the thaw hits, pipes cracked by ice pressure start leaking all at once, overwhelming plumbers and creating backlogs that leave homeowners waiting days.

Severe thunderstorms and tropical weather also drive emergency calls. Heavy rain overwhelms drain systems, reveals roof leaks, and causes hydrostatic pressure to push water through foundation cracks. After major storms, distinguishing between a plumbing leak and weather-related intrusion requires professional investigation — and the answer determines your insurance claim strategy entirely.

The aging plumbing in Houston's older housing stock means sudden pipe failures happen on normal days too. Copper supply lines corroding for 30+ years can fail without warning, turning a quiet evening into an emergency. The older the home, the higher the risk of a sudden catastrophic leak.

We've handled emergency calls during freezes, after tropical storms, and on random Tuesday nights. Whatever the cause, we respond with the same professionalism and thoroughness — just faster.
