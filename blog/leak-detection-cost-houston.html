What's Included in the Price?

When you hire a reputable leak detection company in Houston, your fee should cover a complete investigation — not just someone walking through your house with a single tool. Here's what's included with every Exact Leak Detection HTX inspection:

  • Initial consultation and visual assessment: A thorough walkthrough of the property looking for visible signs of water damage, moisture, staining, and other indicators.
  • Pressure testing: Systematic pressure testing of your supply lines to confirm whether a leak exists and isolate the affected zone.
  • Acoustic leak detection: Electronic listening equipment that amplifies the sound of water escaping from pipes, even through concrete and soil.
  • Thermal imaging: Professional FLIR thermal cameras that detect temperature anomalies caused by hidden moisture and leaking pipes.
  • Moisture mapping: Electronic moisture meters that measure moisture content in floors, walls, and ceilings to trace the path and extent of water damage.
  • Detailed written report: A comprehensive report with findings, photos, thermal images, moisture readings, and recommendations — formatted for homeowners and insurance companies.

This isn't a quick walk-through. A proper leak detection inspection is a systematic, methodical process that uses multiple technologies to confirm and pinpoint the leak's location.

What Affects the Cost of Leak Detection?

While most inspections fall within the price ranges above, several factors can affect the final cost:

Type of Leak

A standard residential leak — like a pipe leaking inside a wall or ceiling — is generally the most straightforward and least expensive to locate. Slab leak detection costs more because it requires additional equipment and time to locate a leak beneath concrete. The technician needs to isolate the system, perform pressure tests, and use acoustic equipment to listen through the slab — a more involved process.

Property Size and Complexity

Commercial properties, multi-story buildings, and large homes take longer to inspect. A 1,500-square-foot house is a different job than a 5,000-square-foot property with multiple bathrooms, a pool, and a sprinkler system. More plumbing means more zones to test and more area to scan.

Number of Leaks

Some inspections reveal more than one leak. An older Houston home with aging copper plumbing might have multiple pinhole leaks in different locations. Each additional leak requires additional investigation time and documentation.

Emergency or After-Hours Service

If you need someone on a Saturday afternoon or have an active water emergency that can't wait until Monday morning, expect to pay an emergency surcharge. At Exact Leak Detection HTX, our emergency and after-hours surcharge is $150 on top of the standard inspection fee.

Accessibility

If the suspected leak area is behind heavy furniture, in a crawl space, or in an area with limited access, the inspection may take longer. We always do our best to work efficiently, but we won't cut corners on thoroughness.

DIY Leak Detection vs. Professional Inspection

Can you find a leak yourself? Sometimes. There are a few things every homeowner can check:

  • Water meter test: Turn off all water-using fixtures and appliances, then check your meter. If it's still moving, you have a leak somewhere.
  • Visual inspection: Look for water stains on ceilings and walls, musty smells, warped flooring, and wet spots in your yard.
  • Toilet dye test: Drop food coloring into your toilet tank. If color appears in the bowl within 15 minutes without flushing, the flapper is leaking.
  • Check exposed pipes: Look under sinks, around water heaters, and at hose bibs for visible drips or corrosion.

These steps can confirm that a leak exists — but they usually can't tell you exactly where it is. That's where professional equipment makes the difference. A thermal camera can see moisture inside a wall. An acoustic device can hear a pinhole leak under six inches of concrete. A pressure test can tell you whether the leak is on your hot side or cold side, your supply line or your drain.

The biggest risk of DIY leak hunting is guessing wrong. If a plumber opens a wall based on a guess and the leak isn't there, you're paying for the exploratory demolition, the repair to close it back up, and then more demolition when they try a different spot. Professional detection almost always saves money in the long run.

When Should You Call a Leak Detection Professional?

Not every drip requires a professional inspection. A leaking faucet or running toilet is usually an obvious fix. But you should call a leak detection specialist when:

  • Your water bill has increased significantly without explanation
  • You hear water running but can't find the source
  • You see water damage (stains, warping, mold) but don't know where it's coming from
  • Your water meter confirms a leak but you can't locate it
  • You suspect a slab leak (hot spots on floor, cracks in foundation)
  • Your insurance company requires a professional leak detection report
  • A plumber has told you they need to "open up" a wall or floor to find the leak — get detection first

In all of these situations, professional leak detection with proper equipment will save you time, money, and stress compared to the alternative.

How to Avoid Overpaying for Leak Detection

Not all leak detection companies are the same. Here are some tips to make sure you're getting fair value:

  1. Get a clear price upfront. Any reputable company should quote you a flat rate or clear price range before they arrive. If they won't give you a number, keep looking.
  2. Ask what's included. Make sure the price includes a written report. Some companies charge extra for documentation — at Exact Leak, it's always included.
  3. Verify their equipment. Ask what tools they use. A company that shows up with just a moisture meter isn't doing a full inspection. Look for thermal imaging, acoustic detection, and pressure testing capabilities.
  4. Check for licensing and insurance. Make sure the company is licensed and insured. This protects you if something goes wrong during the inspection.
  5. Read reviews. Look at Google reviews and ask for references. Consistent positive reviews are the best indicator of quality work.

The Real Cost of Not Detecting a Leak

Here's the reality: the cost of leak detection is a fraction of what an undetected leak can cost you. Consider the potential expenses of ignoring a hidden leak:

  • Water damage repair: $1,000 to $10,000+ for drywall, flooring, and structural repairs
  • Mold remediation: $2,000 to $15,000+ depending on extent
  • Foundation repair: $5,000 to $20,000+ for Houston homes with clay soil
  • Wasted water: Hundreds of dollars per month on your water bill
  • Insurance complications: Delayed detection can result in denied claims if the insurer determines the damage was gradual and preventable

A $325 to $650 investment in professional leak detection can prevent thousands — or tens of thousands — in downstream damage. It's one of the best returns on investment a Houston homeowner can make.
